What Is Survival MD?

Survival MD is a digital survival medicine guide written by Dr. Radu Scurtu, a practicing hospital surgeon with decades of experience in austere environments — from post-communist Eastern Europe (where electricity, medicine, and basic supplies were nonexistent) to modern French hospitals. The book covers how to handle medical emergencies when 911 isn't answering, hospitals are overwhelmed, or you're completely on your own.

What's Inside: Treating wounds, breaks, burns, and injuries; managing chronic disease without medication; stitching cuts; CPR; stockpiling antibiotics legally; the Glasgow Coma Score; delivering a baby; treating radiation poisoning; and 100+ more essential skills.

Best for: Preppers, homesteaders, outdoor enthusiasts, anyone living in remote areas, or families who want to be medically self-reliant in an emergency.

Why Survival Medicine Matters

Understanding why every household needs basic emergency medical knowledge

1. Help May Not Arrive

Studies show we'd need 2.3 million additional health workers just to handle normal demand. In a crisis, hospitals can be overwhelmed, 911 can be unreachable, and the nearest doctor could be hours or days away. Knowing how to treat wounds, manage infections, and stabilize patients is not a hobby — it's a responsibility.

2. Chronic Disease Doesn't Pause

Chronic conditions like diabetes, heart disease, and COPD are the worst killers in a disaster — even more than injuries. When pharmacies close and prescriptions run out, knowing how to manage these conditions naturally can mean the difference between life and death.

3. Knowledge Is Free to Share

Unlike physical supplies that run out, medical knowledge can be shared with neighbors, family, and community members. One person with the right training can multiply their impact by teaching others, creating a more resilient community overall.
